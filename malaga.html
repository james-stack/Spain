Malaga is one of the oldest cities in the world, nearly three thousand years or history within malaga. Malaga was originaly a commercial centre founded by The Phoenicians in 7C Bc, Malaga's original name was "Malaca" derived from the greek word "Malac" meaning to salt. The city was conqured by the Romans in 3C BC the city then became "Flavia Malacita" and remained the centre for commercial importance through the port and many markets that were set up.

Modern Day Malaga

The 1950s were the beginnings of new era for Malaga and the Costa del Sol. Tourism was just beginning to take hold and proving to be an invaluable injection into the local economy. Wandering around Malaga today is like walking around an open air museum with Phoenician, Roman, Moorish and Christian influences. This rich artistic heritage has placed Malaga firmly on the map as the cultural tourist destination for 2014.

Culture

During the celebration of the Feria de Málaga in August, the streets are transformed into traditional symbols of Spanish culture and history, with sweet wine, tapas, and live flamenco shows.

The day events consist of dancing, live music and bullfights at La Malagueta, while the night fair is moved to the Recinto Ferial, consisting of restaurants, clubs, and an entire fair ground with rides and games.
